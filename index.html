Levee authority in turmoil

Since Gov. Jeff Landry took office in 2024, a once-insulated flood-protection agency has seen board members resign, two regional directors come and go, and a police chief’s pay climb to $208,000.

2024
May 20, 2024

Landry declines to advance Norma Jean Mattei.

The governor refuses to forward a unanimously-nominated civil engineer and former head of the American Society of Civil Engineers to the Senate. It is the first public sign of a break with the post-Katrina nominating process.

June 21, 2024

Nominating committee chair warns against politicization.

Jay Lapeyre, who chaired the committee for 12 years, says Landry's refusal "negates the function" of the independent vetting process.

Nov. 6, 2024

Carubba named president; AG investigation opens.

Roy Carubba, who had done engineering work for Shane Guidry's company, is installed as board president. Regional director Kelli Chandler is placed on leave pending an internal probe assisted by the attorney general.

Dec. 13, 2024

Regional director Kelli Chandler resigns.

According to Guidry, the sticking point was the police force: Chandler opposed expanding it; the governor wanted it larger.

2025
Jan. 16, 2025

Guidry's hand becomes visible. "Look at Elon Musk."

The governor's unelected New Orleans adviser emerges as the figure directing agency changes, including a push to elevate the role of the police chief.

March 18, 2025

Four board members quit in protest.

A wave of resignations cites concerns that the agency's flood-protection mission is being diluted. Hurricane season is weeks away.

April 8, 2025

Overhaul bill hits the legislature.

Landry-backed legislation would scrap the independent nominating committee and require a police officer on the board.

May 6, 2025

Overhaul effort fizzles. Bill scaled back.

After legislative pushback, the rewritten bill preserves the nominating committee and most post-Katrina safeguards.

June 17, 2025

"Hit me." A public meeting nearly turns physical.

Shouting, accusations of corruption, and a confrontation between Carubba and Clay Cossé that appeared to nearly turn physical. Guidry said he wasn't aware there was a meeting because he was "on my big yacht."

July 29, 2025

Landry removes Carubba. Vicari installed.

Six weeks after the near-fight, the governor replaces his handpicked president with contractor Peter Vicari. Carubba remains a regular board member.

Sept. 13, 2025

Police budget rises $3.7 million, nearly 50%.

Rondeno claims savings of "millions upon millions" but records show policing spending sharply up.

Oct. 2, 2025

Levee police invite officers to apply for SWAT-trained unit.

An internal document describes specialized training and new rifles for a 50-officer force whose primary mandate is protecting flood infrastructure.

2026
Feb. 3, 2026

Battery allegation against a commissioner.

Rondeno files a misdemeanor battery charge against 68-year-old board member Deborah Settoon, claiming she punched him in the arm. Settoon calls it absurd.

Feb. 4, 2026

Charge dropped the day Settoon resigns.

The police chief withdraws the battery charge after the board member steps down.

April 20, 2026

Police chief's new contract could reach $250,000.

Quietly approved: guaranteed 6% annual raises, firing protection no other employee has, a three-year term ending in 2029. The agency's engineering department is 12 of 18 positions filled.
